Top 10 Fertilizers

Compost

Compost is an organic matter decomposed into a rich, nutrient-dense soil amendment that improves soil fertility and texture.

Manure

Animal manure is a well-known organic fertilizer that provides nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium to the soil. It also improves soil structure.

Bone Meal

Bone meal is a slow-release source of phosphorus and calcium, essential for root development and flowering in plants.

Fish Emulsion

Fish emulsion is a fast-acting, liquid organic fertilizer rich in nitrogen, which promotes healthy growth and green foliage.

Ammonium Nitrate

Ammonium nitrate is a synthetic fertilizer that provides nitrogen to boost plant growth and is commonly used in agricultural settings.

Superphosphate

Superphosphate is a synthetic fertilizer that provides phosphorus, which is crucial for root growth and flower development.

Potassium Chloride

Potassium chloride is a widely used synthetic fertilizer that provides potassium, enhancing overall plant health and resistance to diseases.

Kelp Meal

Kelp meal is an organic fertilizer made from seaweed, packed with micronutrients and trace elements that enhance plant growth and resilience.

Blood Meal

Blood meal is a high-nitrogen organic fertilizer, often used to promote lush foliage growth and enrich soil nitrogen content.

Alfalfa Meal

Alfalfa meal is an organic fertilizer that provides nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, along with trace minerals that improve plant health.
